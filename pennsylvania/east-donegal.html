Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, you’ll typically see permits and regulatory steps tied to what’s being disturbed or removed—not just to “having water in the house.” While PA doesn’t operate with a single, statewide “water damage restoration contractor license” requirement the way some states do, water damage and mold remediation jobs are still governed by overlapping industry standards and environmental and workplace rules. Most restoration work follows IICRC best practices: S500 for water damage and S520 for mold remediation.

For older homes in East Donegal (a meaningful portion built before 1980), you should also watch lead paint requirements. Under the EPA Lead RRP Rule, if a contractor is disturbing paint surfaces in a pre-1978 home, they must have the proper lead-safe setup and certification (you can verify via certifiedrenovatorsearch.epa.gov). Construction demolition that includes lead-painted surfaces isn’t “optional paperwork”—it’s a compliance requirement.

On sewage (Category 3) work, disposal and biohazard handling follow PA DEP Chapter 78 requirements. Building permits are usually only required for specific reconstruction steps—like structural repairs, certain drywall replacement work that involves load-bearing elements, or electrical/plumbing changes done as part of the repair scope—so homeowners should confirm with the local municipality.

Finally, Pennsylvania requires employers to carry workers’ compensation. Before work starts, homeowners should verify coverage (or a valid exemption) using the PA workers’ comp system (WCAIS) and ask for a certificate of insurance naming the homeowner’s address.

Quick credential verification, step-by-step (East Donegal homeowner checklist): (1) Ask for IICRC certification numbers and proof; verify via IICRC/public references when available. (2) Request certificate of insurance (general liability, workers’ comp). (3) For pre-1978 homes: confirm EPA Lead-Safe Certification and the Lead Technician credential for any disturbance of painted surfaces. (4) Confirm workers’ comp proof/exemption via PA WCAIS. (5) Clarify which parts of the job are “mitigation only” versus reconstruction requiring permits, and get that in writing.

How to choose a water damage restoration contractor in East Donegal

What is Category 3 (black water) damage?

Category 3 in East Donegal typically means sewage backup or floodwater that includes significant biohazard risk—often from outside intrusion or sanitary sewer issues. Unlike clean (Category 1) or grey (Category 2) water, Category 3 work generally requires full biohazard protocols: thorough extraction, disinfection, and careful handling/disposal of porous materials that can’t be effectively cleaned (think wet drywall insulation, some subfloor materials, and absorbent contents).
