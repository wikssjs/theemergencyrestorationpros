Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, the rules aren’t all in one place. While homeowners often don’t need permits for drying and non-structural mitigation, water damage and mold-related work can trigger requirements depending on the type of material disturbed and the waste involved. Hempfield homeowners typically see five main regulatory buckets: industry standards, lead-safety, sewage/biohazard disposal, building permits, and workers’ compensation.

First, professional water damage and mold work is commonly guided by IICRC standards—S500 for water damage and S520 for mold—standards most insurers expect when documenting “reasonable and necessary” mitigation. Second, if your Hempfield home was built before 1978, the EPA Lead Renovation, Repair and Painting (RRP) Rule matters whenever contractors disturb lead-painted surfaces. You should confirm the contractor’s EPA Lead-Safe Certification and, where applicable, lead technician documentation (see certifiedrenovatorsearch.epa.gov for verification).

Third, Category 3 projects (sewage backup or storm runoff water) involve biohazard disposal governed by Pennsylvania DEP Chapter 78. Fourth, building permits are typically required for structural repairs (for example, replacing load-influencing drywall sections, framing, or any electrical/plumbing work performed as part of reconstruction). Finally, PA requires employers to carry workers’ compensation; verify coverage or exemption using WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database).

To verify step-by-step: (1) confirm IICRC credentials through the contractor’s documentation; (2) ask for certificate of insurance naming your address; (3) verify EPA Lead-Safe Certification for pre-1978 homes before demolition; and (4) request proof of workers’ comp (or an exemption letter) and confirm in WCAIS.
