Water damage class and category — what Lower Pottsgrove homeowners need to know

In practical terms, a Class 2 loss might be limited to a room and be primarily surface-to-wall saturation, while Class 3 and Class 4 losses can involve wet insulation, deep cavity moisture, and slower-to-dry structural components that require specialty drying and sometimes selective removal.

Here’s a simple dollar example of why this matters: a small Category 1 spill limited to a small area may fall near $800–$5,000 for extraction and emergency drying. Category 3 (sewage/floodwater) then adds biohazard protocols and disposal, so even with similar square footage, the cleanup approach changes.
