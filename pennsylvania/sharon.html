In Sharon’s largely older housing stock (94.7% built before 1980), Class 3 and 4 damage is common after pipe bursts because older plaster and hardwood absorb water differently than modern drywall-only assemblies, meaning moisture can migrate deeper into walls and sub-floors.

Here’s the practical takeaway: Category impacts health risk, PPE, disinfection, and disposal requirements; Class impacts how long materials need to dry to reach drying goals. That’s why a “small leak” can still become expensive if it saturates concealed areas behind finishes, and why a sewage event can cost more even when visible water is limited.
