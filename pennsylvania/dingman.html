Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

In Pennsylvania, water damage and mold-related work is shaped by standards and disposal rules rather than one single “restoration license.” There are several overlapping requirements homeowners should expect contractors to follow. First, the IICRC S500 (water damage) and S520 (mold) standards are the industry baseline for documenting conditions, performing drying, and—when needed—handling microbial contamination. Insurers commonly require work to align with these best-practice protocols.

If your home was built before 1978 and the restoration requires disturbing painted surfaces, the EPA Lead Renovation, Repair and Painting (Lead RRP) Rule can apply. Pennsylvania homeowners should look for an EPA Certified Renovator and ask the contractor how lead-safe practices will be handled. You can search certified renovators at certifiedrenovatorsearch.epa.gov.

For Category 3 sewage or floodwater losses, PA DEP Chapter 78 governs biohazardous/sewage waste disposal for regulated materials. Also remember that building permits may be required for certain repairs—especially if restoration includes structural changes, drywall replacement that affects load-bearing elements, or related electrical and plumbing work. Always check with your local municipality for permit triggers.

Finally, Pennsylvania requires employers to carry workers’ compensation. Homeowners can verify coverage via the WCAIS database (PA workers’ comp). When interviewing contractors in Dingman, ask for: (a) an IICRC certificate of the lead technician (or comparable proof), (b) certificate of insurance naming your address as additional insured when applicable, (c) EPA Lead-Safe Certification for pre-1978 homes, and (d) PA workers’ comp proof or exemption documentation.

Step-by-step: start with online checks for certification, then request a COI and workers’ comp proof before work begins; finally, confirm lead-safe procedures in writing and ask your municipality about permits if electrical/plumbing tie-ins or structural modifications are included.
