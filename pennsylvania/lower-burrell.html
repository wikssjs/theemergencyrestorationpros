Permits & regulations in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania does not require a general contractor license at the state level for every restoration activity, but water damage and mold remediation are still governed by overlapping requirements that affect how work is performed and disposed of. For Lower Burrell homeowners, the most important takeaway is that the “rules” you should verify are about standards, health protocols, lead-safe practices, waste handling, and the permit need for reconstruction—not just whether a crew shows up quickly.

First, the industry baseline in Pennsylvania is the IICRC S500 standard for water damage drying and IICRC S520 for mold. Most insurers expect work to align with these standards, and properly documented drying steps help claims move more smoothly.

Second, if you have a pre-1978 home (many in Lower Burrell are older—matching the county’s historic housing age), the EPA Lead RRP Rule may apply if the contractor disturbs paint on surfaces during repair. Contractors must use an EPA Lead-Safe Certified process; homeowners can verify the certification through the EPA registry at certifiedrenovatorsearch.epa.gov.

Third, PA DEP Chapter 78 governs biohazardous and sewage waste disposal—especially relevant for Category 3 sewage backup cleanups.

Permits typically DO come into play for reconstruction: drywall replacement tied to structural considerations, electrical or plumbing work, or repairs that affect load-bearing elements usually require municipal permits and inspections. Drying, extraction, and non-structural removal generally proceed without building permits, but you should confirm locally with your municipality.

Homeowners should also verify workers’ compensation coverage. Pennsylvania requires employers to carry workers’ comp; check proof or exemption documentation and confirm details via WCAIS (PA workers’ comp database).

When interviewing a company, ask for: (a) IICRC certification documentation/certificate, (b) certificate of insurance naming your address, (c) EPA Lead-Safe Certification for pre-1978 homes, and (d) PA workers’ comp proof or exemption paperwork. Then request the permit-needed scope in writing (what will be dried vs. what will be rebuilt) so you know what triggers inspections in Lower Burrell.
